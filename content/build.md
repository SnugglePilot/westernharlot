I'd been exploring our forestry roads in a track-ready JCW Mini, and the 4″ (102 mm) of clearance wasn't doing me any favours. It was time for a change!

I am a life-long fan of Jeeps, and I love the cockpit on the new Grenadiers. I considered both for a long time. My short-list also included a Jimny and a 4Runner. Each of these was quite capable, but expensive once outfitted. I also live downtown, park in garages, and am not allowed to work on my own vehicle at home. Instead of going big, I started searching for something *small*.

I needed something nimble, high-clearance, low-height, capable on FSR spurs, and able to get me to some pirate campsites. I am often out solo, so extra points for reliability and self-recovery.

That's what led me to the Subaru Crosstrek. The 2024 Wilderness edition specifically; it comes stock with 9.3″ (236 mm) clearance, a CVT cooler, upgraded 2.5L engine, and is re-geared for low-end torque. The Wilderness package also changes the bumpers slightly to increase departure and exit angles. All in a tiny package at an absolute steal of a price. It'll not be able to rock-crawl, but with a few ugrades and the power of reckless momentum it'll be much more capable than it looks.

I christened my 'Trek <span style="color:#ff2d88;">The Western Harlot</span>, after a [climbing pitch in Squamish](https://www.mountainproject.com/route/108370377/western-harlot){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}. Two years and a pile of upgrades later, clearance is up to 11.3″ (287 mm) at the lowest point, and it hasn't let me down yet.
